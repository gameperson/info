The Architect and The Ancestors

This is an exploration of a profound American paradox. Stephen Miller, a primary architect of the most restrictive immigration policies in modern U.S. history, is the descendant of impoverished refugees who fled violent persecution in search of safety. His own family story is a testament to the very immigrant experience his policies seek to curtail. This interactive journey juxtaposes the documented history of his ancestors with the policies he created, revealing a stark and challenging contrast between past and present.

The Ancestors' Journey

Wolf-Lieb & Bessie Glosser

Maternal Great-Great-Grandparents

Fled violent anti-Jewish pogroms in Belarus, arriving at Ellis Island in 1903 with just $8. They spoke no English, only Yiddish, Russian, and Polish.

Their migration was not a choice but a desperate act of survival to escape persecution. Their story embodies the classic refugee experience: arriving with nothing but hope, facing immense language and cultural barriers.

Ruth & Izzy Glosser

Maternal Grandparents

Authored "A Precious Legacy," a family history emphasizing the core value of *tzedakah*â€”charity, justice, and giving back to the needy and persecuted.

The document details how their ancestors began charity work almost immediately upon arriving in the U.S., long before becoming wealthy. This value system, centered on compassion for the vulnerable, was their intended legacy.

Nison (Max) Miller

Paternal Great-Grandfather

Faced bureaucratic hurdles to become a citizen. His first naturalization application in 1932 was denied and marked "ignorant." He persevered and passed on his second attempt.

His story shows that the path to citizenship has long been filled with challenges and subjective judgments, requiring resilience in the face of a complex system.

Jacob "Jay" Miller

Paternal Grandfather

Son of an immigrant, he achieved the American dream, building a small fortune through real estate and law in Los Angeles, securing the family's financial future.

His success represents the upward mobility that immigration can unlock across generations, transforming a family's circumstances within a lifetime.

The Descendant's Policies

Reduced Refugee Admissions & Travel Bans

As a key advisor, Miller championed drastic cuts to refugee admissions and orchestrated travel bans targeting several Muslim-majority countries, directly impacting those fleeing war and persecution.

Contrast: His great-great-grandparents were refugees fleeing religious violence.

Family Separation Policy

Miller was a chief architect of the "zero tolerance" policy that led to the separation of thousands of migrant children from their parents at the U.S. border, a policy justified as a deterrent.

Contrast: His ancestors valued a caring, harmonious family life and aiding the vulnerable.

"Merit-Based" Immigration & Public Charge Rule

Advocated for systems favoring wealthy, highly-educated, and English-speaking immigrants. The "public charge" rule sought to deny green cards to immigrants who might use public benefits.

Contrast: His ancestors arrived impoverished, non-English speaking, and faced a system that could have easily deemed them a "public charge."

Restricting Paths to Citizenship

Pushed for policies that make the naturalization process longer, more expensive, and more difficult, adding new hurdles for legal immigrants seeking to become citizens.

Contrast: His own great-grandfather struggled with and initially failed the citizenship test before succeeding.
